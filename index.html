Deal Assessment
Advantages & Risks
✅ Key Advantages
  • 🔥
    Motivated Seller / JV-Ready
    Seller is desperate at $4M break-even with $2.5M in existing debt. Willingness to JV reduces developer cash to ~$700K for a 95-unit project.
  • 📈
    Land Bump Equity Play
    Locking $6.5M purchase now and capturing CMHC appraisal at entitled value creates $2.0M+ in paper equity at closing — reducing net cash equity by ~54%.
  • 💰
    Minimal Cash Equity
    JV: developer contributes only $700K (18.9%). Land bump: net cash equity ~$1.7M. Extraordinary capital efficiency for a 95-unit build.
  • ⏳
    50-Year Amortization Benefit
    Extended amortization reduces annual debt service by ~$292K vs. 25-year conventional — creating positive Year 1 cash flow vs. ($144K) negative without it.
  • 🏙
    Kingston Road Corridor Momentum
    3,800+ units in active development, City-approved Complete Streets (Dec 2025), and the Kingston Road Revitalization Study support long-term appreciation.
  • ✅
    Zoning Advancement
    OPA/ZBA resubmission #2 filed June 2025. Approved at 4.72 FSI with 10 storeys. Significantly de-risks the entitlement timeline.
⚠️ Risks & Considerations
  • 📉
    Negative Value Spread at $6.5M Land
    Total project cost ($36.33M) slightly exceeds appraised value ($35.98M) by ~$351K. Deal only creates value if land acquired below $6.15M or rents improve.
  • 🎯
    Tight DSCR at 1.10x
    A 5% rent decline compresses DSCR to 1.05x; a 10% decline breaks it below 1.0x. Very limited downside cushion given current market softening.
  • 💸
    Purchase Premium
    The $6.5M break-even price is $1.0M above the seller's own $5.5M valuation. Negotiating closer to $5.5M would significantly improve returns.
  • 📋
    CMHC Premium Increase (July 2025)
    The 4.73% net premium adds ~$1.53M to the mortgage — ~103% higher than pre-July 2025 pricing. Meaningful impact on project economics.
  • 🏘
    Market Softening
    Toronto rents declined 5–12% YoY for 1BR units. Vacancy at 4.2% (highest since 2020). New supply on Kingston Rd. adds competitive pressure.
  • 🔨
    Construction Cost Risk
    The $215/sf assumption needs validation via QS tender pricing for a concrete 10-storey with 2 levels of underground parking in GTA.
Recommended Actions
Next Steps
  • 1
    Negotiate Land Price
    Target $5.5M or below to create positive value spread on completion. At $5.5M (seller's own valuation), the project creates ~$649K in value vs. a negative spread at $6.5M.
  • 2
    Structure JV Term Sheet
    Draft heads of terms including: equity split, cash flow waterfall, promote structure, decision rights, exit mechanisms, and treatment of existing $2.5M debt.
  • 3
    Validate Construction Costs
    Obtain Class C or Class B estimate from a quantity surveyor to confirm $215/sf for concrete mid-rise in GTA.
  • 4
    CMHC Pre-Consultation
    Engage CMHC-approved lender to confirm MLI Select 100-point eligibility and obtain current premium/rate quotes.
  • 5
    Commission Energy Model
    Confirm building can achieve Net-Zero Energy Ready status for 50 energy efficiency points — the largest single point allocation.
  • 6
    Appraisal for Entitled Land Value
    Obtain preliminary appraisal opinion on post-entitlement land value to validate the $8.5M land bump assumption before committing capital.
  • 7
    Track Zoning Approval
    Monitor OPA/ZBA progress following June 2025 resubmission. Confirm timeline to approval and site plan control application.
  • 8
    Engage Construction Lender
    Begin discussions on progress draw financing through the development period in parallel with CMHC mortgage structuring.
